## 🌍 What is uXu?

uXu is a **template and platform for community-curated archives**. Think of it as a digital campground where creators, collectors, and archivists can establish secure trading posts for their own specialized collections—whether that's music, video, research, art, or anything in between.

Each archive operates as a semi-autonomous sub-directory with its own governance, guidelines, and contributor community. Contributors can spawn new archives or collaborate on existing ones.

## 🚀 Starting Your Own Archive

Want to create a new archive within uXu? Follow this structure:

```
/archives/
  /YourArchiveName/
    /README.md           # Archive description & guidelines
    /LICENSE             # Archive-specific license
    /CONTRIBUTORS.md     # Archive team & credits
    /assets/             # Main content directory
    /docs/               # Archive documentation
```

### Quick Start Archive Template

1. **Fork or create a new folder** in `/archives/` with your archive name.
2. **Write a README** explaining your archive's theme, content types, and contribution guidelines.
3. **Choose a license** (CC BY-SA, CC0, MIT, or other).
4. **Open a PR** to add your archive to the uXu registry.
5. **Invite collaborators** and start building community around your collection.

## 🛡️ Core Community Principles

All archives within uXu must adhere to these foundational principles:

### 1. **Legal & Ethical First**
- Contributors must hold distribution rights or have explicit permission for all content.
- No pirated software, malware, or illegally sourced materials.
- Respect copyright and intellectual property boundaries.

### 2. **Content Transparency**
- NSFW or sensitive content must be clearly labeled and isolated in a `/NSFW` folder.
- Include content warnings in archive READMEs.
- Maintain clear metadata on content origins and licensing.

### 3. **Inclusive Bias-Neutral Curation**
- Archives should welcome diverse content types, perspectives, and communities.
- Avoid gatekeeping; let the archive curator set tone while remaining open to collaboration.
- Celebrate different styles, genres, and archival approaches.

### 4. **Collaborative Spirit**
- "Strangers stopping strangers, just to shake their hand."
- View contributors as creative peers, not competitors.
- Foster respectful discussion and welcome constructive feedback.

## 🤝 How to Contribute

### To an Existing Archive:
1. **Fork** this repository to your GitHub account.
2. **Navigate** to the archive you wish to contribute to (e.g., `/archives/CyberCat-Sunflower/`).
3. **Add or update files** in the archive's `/assets/` or `/docs/` directory.
4. **Open a Pull Request** with a clear description of your contribution.
5. **Engage** with the archive curator and community for feedback.

### To Create a New Archive:
1. **Fork** this repository.
2. **Create a new directory** under `/archives/` with your archive name.
3. **Add a README.md** describing your archive (theme, content types, guidelines).
4. **Add a LICENSE** file specifying how your archive content can be used.
5. **Open a PR** to register your archive with the uXu community.

## 📋 Guidelines for Archive Curation

Each archive curator should define:
- **Content Scope:** What types of files/media does this archive accept?
- **Quality Standards:** Are there submission requirements (format, metadata, etc.)?
- **Collaboration Model:** How should contributors interact? (Discord, discussions, issues?)
- **Archival Philosophy:** What makes this collection special or unique?
